# Employee Stock Option Plan (ESOP) Policy

## 1. Purpose

This Employee Stock Option Plan (ESOP) Policy outlines the framework for granting stock options to eligible employees of Acme Corp. The purpose of this policy is to align employee interests with those of the company's shareholders, incentivize long-term commitment and performance, and attract and retain key talent.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all eligible employees of Acme Corp, as determined by the Board of Directors or its authorized committee.

## 3. Eligibility

Eligibility for participation in the ESOP will be determined based on factors such as:
*   Employee's role and level within the organization.
*   Length of service with Acme Corp.
*   Performance and contribution to the company's success.
*   Any other criteria deemed appropriate by the Board of Directors.

## 4. Grant of Stock Options

Stock options will be granted at the discretion of the Board of Directors or its authorized committee. The terms and conditions of each option grant, including the number of options, exercise price, vesting schedule, and expiration date, will be detailed in a separate Stock Option Agreement provided to the eligible employee.

## 5. Vesting Schedule

Vesting of stock options will be subject to a predetermined schedule, typically based on continued employment with Acme Corp. The vesting schedule will be clearly defined in the Stock Option Agreement.

## 6. Exercise of Stock Options

Eligible employees will have the right to exercise their vested stock options within a specified period, as outlined in their Stock Option Agreement. The exercise price will be determined at the time of the grant.

## 7. Termination of Employment

The treatment of unvested and vested stock options upon termination of employment (voluntary or involuntary) will be governed by the terms specified in the Stock Option Agreement and applicable laws.

## 8. Administration

The administration of the ESOP will be overseen by the Human Resources department, in conjunction with the Finance department and the Board of Directors or its authorized committee.

## 9. Confidentiality

All information related to ESOP grants, individual option holdings, and related financial matters is considered confidential and should not be disclosed to unauthorized individuals.

## 10. Compliance

The ESOP will be administered in compliance with all applicable Indian laws and regulations, including but not limited to the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) guidelines and the Companies Act, 2013.

## 11. Policy Review

This ESOP Policy will be reviewed periodically by the Board of Directors or its authorized committee and may be amended as necessary to ensure its continued effectiveness and compliance with legal requirements and business objectives.

## 12. Disclaimer

This policy provides a general framework for the ESOP. Specific terms and conditions for individual grants will be detailed in the Stock Option Agreement. Employees are encouraged to consult with their financial advisor regarding the tax implications of stock options.
