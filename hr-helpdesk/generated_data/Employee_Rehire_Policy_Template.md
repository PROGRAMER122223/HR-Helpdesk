# Employee Stock Ownership Plan (ESOP) Policy

## 1. Purpose

This Employee Stock Ownership Plan (ESOP) Policy outlines the framework and guidelines for the establishment and administration of an ESOP at Acme Corp. The ESOP is designed to provide eligible employees with an opportunity to acquire ownership in the company, thereby aligning employee interests with the company's long-term success and fostering a culture of shared ownership and commitment.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all eligible employees of Acme Corp. and its subsidiaries, as defined within this policy. It also governs the responsibilities of the ESOP trustee, plan administrator, and other relevant parties involved in the ESOP's operation.

## 3. ESOP Committee

Acme Corp. shall establish an ESOP Committee, comprising representatives from senior management, Human Resources, and Finance. The ESOP Committee will be responsible for:

*   Overseeing the administration and operation of the ESOP.
*   Ensuring compliance with all applicable laws and regulations, including the Employee Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952, and any relevant tax laws in India.
*   Reviewing and approving ESOP grants and allocations.
*   Communicating ESOP-related information to employees.
*   Making recommendations for amendments to the ESOP policy.

## 4. Eligibility

All full-time employees of Acme Corp. who have completed [Specify number, e.g., one year] of continuous service with the company are eligible to participate in the ESOP. Part-time employees and contract staff are not eligible for participation. Eligibility criteria may be further defined for specific grant cycles or employee levels as determined by the ESOP Committee.

## 5. ESOP Grant and Allocation

*   **Granting Authority:** The ESOP Committee, with the approval of the Board of Directors, shall have the authority to grant ESOP options or awards to eligible employees.
*   **Grant Size:** The number of ESOP options or shares allocated to an employee will be determined based on factors such as employee grade, performance, tenure, and the overall ESOP pool available.
*   **Vesting Schedule:** ESOP grants will be subject to a vesting schedule, typically over a period of [Specify number, e.g., four years], with a cliff vesting period of [Specify number, e.g., one year]. This means that a portion of the granted options/shares will become exercisable or owned by the employee only after completing a specified period of service. The vesting schedule will be clearly communicated to the employee at the time of the grant.
*   **Exercise Period:** Upon vesting, employees will have a defined period to exercise their vested options, typically [Specify number, e.g., ten years] from the date of the grant.

## 6. Exercise of Options and Share Purchase

*   **Exercise Process:** Vested employees wishing to exercise their ESOP options must submit a formal request to the ESOP Committee or designated administrator.
*   **Purchase Price:** The exercise price for ESOP options will be determined at the time of the grant, typically at the fair market value of the company's shares on the grant date.
*   **Payment:** Employees will be responsible for paying the exercise price for the shares they wish to purchase. Payment methods and timelines will be communicated by the ESOP administrator.

## 7. Termination of Employment

*   **Vested Options:** Upon termination of employment (voluntary or involuntary), vested ESOP options must typically be exercised within a specified period, usually [Specify number, e.g., 90 days] from the last day of employment. Failure to exercise vested options within this period may result in their forfeiture.
*   **Unvested Options:** Unvested ESOP options will be forfeited upon termination of employment.
*   **Special Circumstances:** In cases of retirement, death, or permanent disability, specific provisions may apply regarding the exercise of vested options, as determined by the ESOP Committee and in accordance with applicable laws.

## 8. Valuation and Reporting

*   **Share Valuation:** The fair market value of Acme Corp. shares will be determined periodically by an independent third-party valuation expert, in accordance with applicable regulations.
*   **Reporting:** Employees will receive regular statements detailing their ESOP grants, vesting status, and any relevant financial information.

## 9. Tax Implications

Employees are solely responsible for any taxes arising from the grant, vesting, exercise, or sale of ESOP shares. Acme Corp. will comply with all applicable tax reporting requirements in India, including providing necessary documentation to employees and tax authorities. Employees are strongly advised to consult with their own tax advisors regarding the tax implications of their ESOP participation.

## 10. Confidentiality

All information related to an employee's ESOP participation, including grant details, vesting schedules, and financial data, is confidential and should not be disclosed to third parties without the company's prior written consent.

## 11. Amendments and Termination of the ESOP

Acme Corp. reserves the right to amend, modify, or terminate the ESOP policy and the ESOP plan at any time, for any reason, in accordance with applicable laws and regulations. Employees will be notified of any such changes.

## 12. Governing Law

This ESOP Policy and the ESOP plan shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of India.

## 13. Disclaimer

This policy provides a general overview of the ESOP at Acme Corp. The specific terms and conditions of the ESOP will be detailed in the formal ESOP Plan Document and grant agreements. In case of any conflict between this policy and the ESOP Plan Document or applicable laws, the ESOP Plan Document and applicable laws shall prevail. Employees are encouraged to review the ESOP Plan Document and consult with the ESOP Committee or their designated administrator for any clarifications.
