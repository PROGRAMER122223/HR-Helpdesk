## Employee Stock Ownership Plan (ESOP) Policy - Acme Corp

**1. Policy Statement**

Acme Corp is committed to fostering a culture of ownership and rewarding the dedication and contributions of its employees. The Employee Stock Ownership Plan (ESOP) is designed to provide eligible employees with the opportunity to share in the company's success through the ownership of company stock. This policy outlines the terms, conditions, and procedures governing the ESOP.

**2. Purpose**

The primary purposes of the ESOP are to:

*   **Align Employee and Company Interests:** Encourage employees to think and act like owners by linking their financial interests directly to the company's performance and growth.
*   **Reward Long-Term Commitment:** Recognize and reward employees for their sustained contributions and loyalty to Acme Corp.
*   **Enhance Employee Motivation and Retention:** Provide a tangible incentive for employees to perform at their best and remain with the company.
*   **Attract Top Talent:** Serve as a competitive benefit to attract and recruit highly skilled individuals.

**3. Scope**

This policy applies to all eligible employees of Acme Corp and its subsidiaries, as defined in Section 5 of this policy.

**4. ESOP Administration**

The ESOP will be administered by a designated ESOP Trustee or Committee, appointed by the Board of Directors. The Administrator(s) will be responsible for:

*   Overseeing the day-to-day operations of the ESOP.
*   Ensuring compliance with all applicable laws and regulations, including the Companies Act, 2013, and any relevant SEBI guidelines.
*   Managing the allocation and distribution of shares.
*   Communicating ESOP details to eligible employees.
*   Maintaining accurate records related to the ESOP.

**5. Eligibility**

*   **General Eligibility:** All full-time employees who have completed [Specify Number, e.g., one (1) year] of continuous service with Acme Corp are eligible to participate in the ESOP.
*   **Service Requirement:** Continuous service is defined as uninterrupted employment with Acme Corp, excluding any periods of unpaid leave exceeding [Specify Number, e.g., thirty (30)] days, unless otherwise approved by the ESOP Administrator.
*   **Exclusions:** Employees on fixed-term contracts, consultants, temporary staff, and employees who have resigned or been terminated for cause prior to the grant date or vesting date (as applicable) are generally not eligible. Specific exclusions may be detailed in the ESOP Grant Agreement.

**6. Grant of Options/Awards**

*   **Grant Committee:** The Board of Directors, or a committee designated by the Board (the "Grant Committee"), will determine the eligibility of employees for ESOP grants, the number of options or awards granted, and the grant price (if applicable).
*   **Grant Agreement:** Each eligible employee receiving an ESOP grant will be provided with an ESOP Grant Agreement, which will detail the specific terms of their grant, including the number of options/awards, exercise price (if applicable), vesting schedule, and expiration date.

**7. Vesting Schedule**

*   **Vesting:** ESOP grants will vest according to a predetermined schedule outlined in the ESOP Grant Agreement. A typical vesting schedule might be:
    *   [Specify Vesting Schedule, e.g., 25% of the options/awards vest after one year of service, with the remaining vesting in equal installments over the subsequent three years, on each anniversary of the grant date.]
*   **Vesting Upon Certain Events:** The ESOP Grant Agreement may specify accelerated vesting upon certain events, such as a Change in Control of Acme Corp or the employee's death or permanent disability.

**8. Exercise of Options/Awards**

*   **Exercise Period:** Vested options or awards may be exercised by the employee within a specified period, as detailed in the ESOP Grant Agreement.
*   **Exercise Price:** If applicable, the exercise price will be determined by the Grant Committee at the time of the grant, typically based on the fair market value of the company's stock.
*   **Exercise Procedure:** Employees will be provided with instructions on how to exercise their vested options or awards.

**9. Termination of Employment**

*   **Resignation:** If an employee resigns, any unvested options or awards will be forfeited. Vested options or awards must typically be exercised within a specified period (e.g., 90 days) following the effective date of resignation, as per the ESOP Grant Agreement.
*   **Termination for Cause:** If employment is terminated for cause (e.g., gross misconduct, fraud, violation of company policy), all vested and unvested options or awards may be forfeited, as determined by the Grant Committee and in accordance with applicable law.
*   **Termination Without Cause:** If employment is terminated without cause, vested options or awards must typically be exercised within a specified period following the effective date of termination, as per the ESOP Grant Agreement. Unvested options or awards will be forfeited.
*   **Death or Disability:** In the event of an employee's death or permanent disability, the ESOP Grant Agreement may provide for accelerated vesting and/or an extended exercise period for vested options or awards.

**10. Tax Implications**

*   Employees are responsible for understanding and complying with all applicable tax laws related to their ESOP grants, including income tax, capital gains tax, and any other relevant taxes in India.
*   Acme Corp will provide employees with necessary documentation related to their ESOP grants for tax purposes, as required by law. Employees are encouraged to consult with their own tax advisors.

**11. Confidentiality**

All information regarding the ESOP, including grant details, vesting schedules, and personal financial information, is confidential and should not be disclosed to any third party without the express written consent of Acme Corp, except as required by law.

**12. Policy Amendments**

Acme Corp reserves the right to amend, modify, or terminate the ESOP policy and any individual ESOP grants at any time, in accordance with applicable laws and regulations. Any significant changes will be communicated to eligible employees.

**13. Governing Law**

This ESOP Policy and all ESOP grants shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of India, including but not limited to the Companies Act, 2013, and any relevant SEBI regulations.

**14. Disclaimer**

This policy provides a general overview of the ESOP. The specific terms and conditions of each employee's participation will be governed by their individual ESOP Grant Agreement and the formal ESOP Plan document. In the event of any conflict between this policy and the ESOP Plan document or Grant Agreement, the terms of the ESOP Plan document and Grant Agreement shall prevail. Employees are encouraged to review the full ESOP Plan document and consult with the ESOP Administrator or their own legal and financial advisors for detailed information.
