# Acme Corp Information Technology Assets Policy

## Objective

This policy establishes guidelines for the responsible use, management, and protection of all information technology (IT) assets owned by Acme Corp. Its purpose is to safeguard company data, prevent unauthorized access or misuse, ensure operational continuity, and maintain compliance with relevant regulations.

## Scope and Applicability

This policy applies to all individuals who access, utilize, or are responsible for Acme Corp's IT assets. This includes, but is not limited to, all employees, contractors, consultants, temporary staff, and any other personnel affiliated with third-party organizations who interact with Acme Corp's IT infrastructure and resources.

## Definitions

| Term / Abbreviation | Definition / Expansion |
|---|---|
| **IT Asset** | Any hardware, software, data, network infrastructure, or digital resource owned or licensed by Acme Corp. |
| **User** | Any individual covered by the Scope and Applicability section of this policy. |
| **Data** | Any information, in any format, created, processed, stored, or transmitted using Acme Corp IT assets. |
| **Information Security** | The protection of information from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction. |

## Policy / Process

### 1. IT Asset Inventory and Management

*   All IT assets procured by Acme Corp must be acquired through the designated IT Procurement process.
*   Each IT asset will be assigned a unique identifier and recorded in the central IT Asset Register.
*   The IT Asset Register will maintain comprehensive details, including:
    *   Asset Type (e.g., Laptop, Server, Software License, Mobile Device)
    *   Manufacturer and Model
    *   Serial Number / Unique Identifier
    *   Assigned User / Department
    *   Location
    *   Purchase Date and Cost
    *   Warranty Information
    *   Software Licenses Associated (where applicable)

### 2. Ownership and Accountability

*   All IT assets are the exclusive property of Acme Corp.
*   Users are entrusted with the care and responsible use of assigned IT assets. This entrustment does not transfer ownership.
*   Users are accountable for:
    *   Protecting assigned IT assets from damage, loss, theft, or unauthorized access.
    *   Ensuring that all data handled on company IT assets is classified and protected according to Acme Corp's Data Protection Policy.
    *   Adhering to all security protocols and configurations for electronic equipment.

### 3. Acceptable Use of IT Assets

*   IT assets are primarily intended for business-related activities. Limited personal use may be permitted, provided it does not interfere with job responsibilities, violate any company policies, or incur additional costs for Acme Corp.
*   Users must exercise good judgment and discretion when using company IT assets, including internet and email services.
*   Users are prohibited from:
    *   Installing unauthorized software.
    *   Accessing or attempting to access systems or data for which they do not have explicit authorization.
    *   Sharing login credentials.
    *   Engaging in any illegal or unethical activities.
    *   Circumventing security measures.
*   All data created or stored on Acme Corp IT assets is considered company property.

### 4. Security and Monitoring

*   Users are responsible for the security of their assigned devices and the data they contain. This includes implementing strong passwords, locking devices when unattended, and reporting any suspected security incidents immediately.
*   Acme Corp reserves the right to monitor the use of its IT assets, including network traffic, system activity, and data stored on company equipment, to ensure compliance with this policy and for security purposes. Such monitoring will be conducted in accordance with applicable laws and regulations.

### 5. Return of IT Assets

*   Upon termination of employment, contract, or any other agreement with Acme Corp, all assigned IT assets must be returned promptly to the IT Department or their designated representative.
*   This includes, but is not limited to, laptops, mobile devices, access cards, software, and any storage media containing company data.
*   Users must ensure that any personal data is securely removed from devices before return, following IT Department guidelines. Acme Corp is not responsible for the loss of personal data due to improper removal.

## Special Circumstances and Exceptions

Any exceptions to this policy require formal written approval from the Head of IT and the relevant Department Head.

## Non-compliance and Consequences

Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or contract, and may also lead to legal or financial repercussions.
