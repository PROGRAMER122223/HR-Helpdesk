# Acme Corp Employee Expense Reimbursement Policy

## POLICY OVERVIEW AND OBJECTIVE

Acme Corp provides company-issued credit cards to facilitate necessary business expenditures for employees. This policy establishes clear guidelines for the responsible use of these cards, ensuring compliance, financial accountability, and efficient management of company resources.

The primary objectives of this policy are to:
*   Define eligibility criteria and the authorization process for company credit cards.
*   Outline the terms and conditions governing the use of company credit cards.
*   Specify spending limits, permissible expenses, and prohibited uses.
*   Detail the procedures for reporting and reconciling expenses, and the consequences of policy violations.

## APPLICABILITY

This policy applies to all Acme Corp employees who are issued a company credit card or are authorized to incur expenses on behalf of the company using a company credit card.

## AUTHORIZATION AND ISSUANCE

Company credit cards are issued at the discretion of the Finance Department, with approval from the Head of Department and the Chief Financial Officer (CFO). Eligibility is typically based on job function, travel frequency, and the need to incur business expenses. All cardholders must undergo a background check and agree to the terms outlined in this policy.

## EMPLOYEE APPLICATION AND AGREEMENT

Employees requiring a company credit card must submit a formal "Company Credit Card Application" form, detailing the business need and expected usage. This application requires departmental and financial approval.

Upon approval, the employee must sign a "Company Credit Cardholder Agreement." This agreement signifies the employee's understanding and acceptance of their responsibilities, including:
*   The card remains the property of Acme Corp.
*   The card is for business use only.
*   The cardholder is responsible for all authorized charges.
*   Acme Corp reserves the right to monitor and audit card usage.

## CONDITIONS OF USE

*   **Permissible Expenses:** Company credit cards are to be used solely for legitimate business expenses directly related to an employee's role and responsibilities. This includes, but is not limited to, approved travel, accommodation, client entertainment, and necessary business supplies. Refer to the Acme Corp Travel and Entertainment Policy for specific guidelines.
*   **Prohibited Expenses:** The use of company credit cards for personal expenses, cash advances, gambling, or any illegal or unethical activities is strictly prohibited.
*   **Card Security:** Cardholders are responsible for the safekeeping of their company credit card. The card must not be shared with any other individual, including colleagues.
*   **Reporting:** All transactions must be reported and reconciled through the company's expense management system within five (5) business days of the transaction date. Supporting documentation (receipts, invoices) must be attached.
*   **Audits:** Acme Corp reserves the right to audit all company credit card statements and associated expenses at any time.
*   **Travel:** When traveling on company business, the credit card may be used for approved travel, lodging, and meal expenses in accordance with the Acme Corp Travel and Entertainment Policy.

## EXCEPTIONS AND WAIVERS

Any request for expenses exceeding the established spending limits must be pre-approved by the employee's direct manager and the CFO. Such requests must include a detailed justification for the additional expenditure.

## DISPUTED TRANSACTIONS

If a cardholder disputes a charge on their company credit card, they must notify the Finance Department immediately and provide all relevant details and supporting documentation. The Finance Department will work with the card issuer and the merchant to resolve the dispute. The cardholder is responsible for providing timely information to facilitate this process.

## POLICY VIOLATIONS AND DISCIPLINARY ACTION

Failure to adhere to this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment. Examples of violations include, but are not limited to:
*   Using the company credit card for personal or unauthorized expenses.
*   Sharing the company credit card with unauthorized individuals.
*   Failure to submit expense reports and supporting documentation in a timely manner.
*   Making fraudulent or illegal charges.
*   Repeatedly exceeding spending limits without proper authorization.

The severity of disciplinary action will be determined by the Finance Department and Human Resources, considering the nature, frequency, and impact of the violation.
