# Acme Corp - Acceptable Use Policy for Company-Provided Digital Resources

## 1. Policy Statement and Objectives

Acme Corp is committed to providing its employees with the necessary digital tools to perform their job functions effectively. This policy outlines the acceptable use of company-provided digital resources, including but not limited to, email, internet access, and internal communication platforms.

The primary objectives of this policy are to:
*   Ensure the efficient and professional use of company digital assets.
*   Protect Acme Corp's confidential information, intellectual property, and reputation.
*   Maintain a secure and productive work environment.
*   Comply with all applicable Indian laws and regulations.

## 2. Scope of Policy

This policy applies to all Acme Corp employees, contractors, temporary staff, and any third parties who are granted access to Acme Corp's digital resources, regardless of their location or device used to access these resources.

## 3. Acceptable Use of Digital Resources

Company-provided digital resources are intended for business-related activities. While limited personal use may be permitted, it must not interfere with job duties, consume excessive resources, or violate any other company policies.

### 3.1. Email Usage

Company email accounts are provided for communication related to Acme Corp's business. This includes:
*   Communicating with clients, partners, and vendors.
*   Accessing necessary business software and services.
*   Registering for industry-relevant conferences, webinars, and professional development opportunities.
*   Sharing work-related information and updates internally.

### 3.2. Internet Usage

Employees may use company internet access for business purposes, including research, accessing cloud-based applications, and professional networking. Limited personal use is permissible, provided it does not:
*   Access illegal, offensive, or inappropriate content.
*   Interfere with network performance or security.
*   Violate any other company policies.

### 3.3. Internal Communication Platforms

Acme Corp's internal communication tools (e.g., instant messaging, collaboration software) are primarily for business discussions. Professional conduct and respectful communication are expected at all times.

## 4. Prohibited Use of Digital Resources

The following activities are strictly prohibited when using Acme Corp's digital resources:

*   **Illegal Activities:** Engaging in or facilitating any illegal activity, including copyright infringement, fraud, or harassment.
*   **Offensive Content:** Sending, accessing, or distributing content that is discriminatory, defamatory, obscene, harassing, or otherwise offensive.
*   **Unauthorized Access:** Attempting to gain unauthorized access to company systems, data, or the digital resources of others.
*   **Security Violations:** Introducing malware, viruses, or other harmful software; attempting to bypass security measures; or sharing login credentials.
*   **Commercial Solicitation:** Using company email or platforms for personal commercial gain, unauthorized advertising, or chain letters, unless explicitly approved for business purposes.
*   **Misrepresentation:** Impersonating others or misrepresenting your affiliation with Acme Corp.
*   **Excessive Personal Use:** Using company resources for extensive personal activities that impact productivity or network resources.
*   **Confidentiality Breaches:** Disclosing or attempting to disclose confidential Acme Corp information without proper authorization.

## 5. Security Measures and Employee Responsibilities

Employees are responsible for safeguarding company digital resources and data. This includes:

*   **Password Management:** Creating strong, unique passwords for all company accounts. Passwords should be at least 12 characters long, combining uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using easily guessable information like birthdays or names. Passwords should not be shared.
*   **Phishing and Malware Awareness:** Exercising caution with unsolicited emails, attachments, and links. Employees must verify the sender's identity and scrutinize email content for suspicious signs (e.g., poor grammar, unusual requests, generic greetings). Report any suspected phishing attempts or malware to the IT Security department immediately.
*   **Software Updates:** Ensuring that company-provided devices and software are kept up-to-date with the latest security patches.
*   **Data Protection:** Handling all company data, especially sensitive or confidential information, with the utmost care and in accordance with Acme Corp's Data Protection Policy.

## 6. Monitoring and Auditing

Acme Corp reserves the right to monitor, access, and audit the use of its digital resources, including email and internet activity, to ensure compliance with this policy, protect company assets, and investigate potential policy violations. Employees should have no expectation of privacy when using company-provided digital resources.

## 7. Email Signatures

All employees are required to use a standardized company email signature for professional communication. The signature should include:

*   Employee Name
*   Job Title
*   Acme Corp
*   Company Website (e.g., www.acmecorp.in)
*   Contact Number (Optional, if applicable)

Branding guidelines for email signatures, including logo usage, can be found on the company intranet or by contacting the Marketing department.

## 8. Policy Violations and Disciplinary Action

Failure to comply with this Acceptable Use Policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment. Depending on the severity of the violation, actions may include:

*   Verbal or written warnings.
*   Suspension or revocation of access to digital resources.
*   Mandatory security awareness training.
*   Termination of employment.
*   Legal action, where applicable.

Acme Corp reserves the right to take appropriate action based on the nature and impact of the policy violation.
